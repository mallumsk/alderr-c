activPractice | Why Transactional Billing Wins

A Billing Model Built on Volume, Accuracy, and Accountability

Traditional billing ties fees to a percentage of revenue, hiding operational performance behind financial outcomes. activPractice rebuilds billing around the transaction itself—every claim, every adjustment, every payment—so practices finally see clear, measurable performance across the entire revenue cycle.

Charge Capture, Scrubbing, Rejections, Posting—All Bundled Into One Transparent Cost

Under a percentage-of-collections model, each step of the revenue cycle feels disconnected. activPractice unifies the core billing tasks—charge entry, scrubber review, clearinghouse rejections, payment posting—into a single per-claim price. Providers see exactly what it costs to move a claim cleanly through the system, without surprises, markups, or hidden inefficiencies.

Built on Real Volume, Not Revenue Fluctuations

A per-claim model reflects the true work being done. Instead of billing based on dollar value—which can distort incentives and obscure operational clarity—activPractice charges based on volume.

The economics become simple:

  • How many claims did we submit?
  • How quickly were they completed?
  • How accurately were they completed?
  • Practices finally gain a billing cost structure that matches operational reality.

Button

Predictive Modeling for AR and Rejection Work

Not every claim moves cleanly through the workflow. Some portion will fall into rejections, clarifications, or AR follow-up. activPractice models these categories upfront and builds their expected cost into the per-claim rate. This gives practices stable pricing and gives Alderr a direct incentive to minimize downstream work. When AR touches increase or rejection rates spike, we don’t hide behind percentages—we fix the operational issue.

Button

The Logical Cascade of KPIs

With transactional billing, performance becomes transparent and measurable:

  • Was the claim completed once?

    Target: 24-hour turnaround.

  • Was it completed correctly?

    Target: <2% error rate.

  • Was the first-pass resolution rate strong?

    Poor FPRR creates hidden downstream costs that erode Alderr's margins.

  • Was AR work necessary, and was it resolved on the first touch?

    Every unnecessary touch increases cost and decreases the probability of collection.

This model exposes the truth: the only way Alderr wins is by doing the work right the first time.
